# Conclusion

The Apollo 13 accident serves as a testament to the ingenuity, expertise, and dedication of the mission control team at NASA. By studying the intricate details of the spacecraft's power system and the real-time debugging efforts of the ground crew, we gain a deeper appreciation for the challenges faced during this critical moment in space exploration history.

The examination of an actual Apollo Command Module inverter provides valuable insights into the design and components of the electrical system. However, it also highlights the importance of proper maintenance and storage of these historical artifacts. The discovery of hazardous materials, such as vermiculite and asbestos, inside the inverter underscores the need for caution and safety when handling vintage aerospace equipment.
